The Loft

Visit Prototype

Project Background:

The cloud storage industry is booming along with the increasing need to collaborate and work remotely. Connect is a new innovative cloud platform well suited for storing and sharing data from anywhere in the world.

Objective:

Creating a desktop site where files can be stored, shared, and accessed anytime, from anywhere.

My Role:

UX Researcher, UX Designer

Project Duration:

4 weeks

Tools:

Figma, Google Forms, Google Sheets

Problems

  • The number of coronavirus cases and deaths are on the rise.
  • The economy is suffering as a result of businesses being forced to shut down.
  • There has been an increase in crime rate and mental health illnesses.
  • Businesses have asked their employees to work remotely from home.
  • Many people are canceling group gatherings to comply with CDC’s recommendation of social distancing.

Solution

Design a video conferencing platform that is user-friendly and full of interesting features that benefits businesses and the overall population.

Process

I really enjoyed working on this project because it involved designing a platform that could potentially help mend broken bridges caused by the coronavirus pandemic. I wanted to make sure the site was feasible and versatile, making it useful to both business owners and the overall population. Prior to sketching ideas, I devoted a good chunk of my time to research. Once I learned about the current competitors and users, I began to flesh out the different wireframes for The Loft. Then conducted usability testings that would allow potential users to interact with the design. The results collected were then used to modify the design, and ultimately improve the overall function of The Loft. Let me walk you through the different stages.

User Surveys

Before I could start the design process, I needed to collect some valuable data via user surveys. Twenty-one individuals participated in this exercise. The data collected allowed me to evaluate whether or not there truly is a need for a video conferencing platform, and in what ways people care to use it. I anticipated the top two leading video conferencing platforms would have been Zoom and FaceTime. Unfortunately, the top two frequently used video conferencing platforms among the 21 participants were Zoom (100%) and Skype (76.2%). I predicted Zoom would be the main video conferencing platform used for personal use and the Cisco WebEx would be the main platform used for work. However, Zoom turned out to be the leading video conferencing platform for both personal use and work. As expected, there has been an increase in the use of video conferencing platforms since the rise of Covid-19, which supports the growing need for a video conferencing platform such as The Loft.

Results

  • Before the start of Covid-19, roughly 24% of participants use video conferencing platforms for more than an hour.
  • After the rise of Covid-19, roughly 67% of participants use video conferencing platforms for more than an hour.
  • The top 3 features: sharing screens (76.2%), mute feature (52.4%), turn off camera (52.4%).
  • The top 3 video conferencing platforms are Zoom, Skype, and Google Hangouts
  • 100% of participants do not pay a monthly fee to use their video conferencing platform
  • 71.4% of participants use Zoom and 14.3% of participants use Skype for personal use.
  • 52.4% of participants use Zoom and 19% of participants use Cisco WebEx/Appear.in at work.
  • 95.2% of participants use a laptop to access a video conferencing platform

Take Away Message

Users value free storage space on the cloud platform. They mostly use the platform for personal use and/or work. Many users share pictures and documents with other people through cloud platforms.

Competitive Analysis

Next, I conducted a competitive analysis on the three most popular video conferencing platforms among the survey participants: Zoom, Skype, and Google Hangouts.

If I had more time, I would have included at least two more competitors to the list. Since I use a few of these platforms myself, I was familiar with how they typically work.

However, I wish I also had more time to navigate on other video conferencing sites to see how each organized data on a page.

Plan of Action

Ways to set the new cloud storage apart that stands out from its competitors:


  • User friendly
  • Larger amount of free storage space (50GB)
  • Easy to share files and collaborate in real-time
  • All features are made readily available to all users no matter their storage plan

Usability Testing I

Once I finished the digital wireframes in Figma, I was able to create clickable prototypes in order to perform usability testing on potential users. Below are the 5 tasks observed during the usability testing.

1) Sign up for an account using email address and password

2) Log out from the homepage and then log in using email address and password

3) Schedule a meeting using the participant’s email address

4) Mute yourself (name: Derrick Brown) during a conference call

5) Share screen 2 with colleagues

In the beginning, I predicted the participants would have difficulties with scheduling a meeting. However, that was not the case. In fact, task 1,2,3,& 5 were completed successfully on the first try. On task 4, one participant clicked the microphone found in the right sidebar beside the name Derrick Brown instead of selecting the microphone button located at the bottom. This was no mistake of the participants but rather the designer.

Lessons Learned
from Usability Testing

Although I anticipated the onboarding task to be the easiest of the three tasks, I was surprised to observe the users struggling to find the right button to click on the landing page. The “Try it free” button was placed on the landing page as a way to motivate new users to sign up for a free account. However, this button did not indicate that it was the sign up button. Instead, it caused a lot of confusion, and the participants clicked on different options before selecting the correct button.

Updates Made to Wireframe

  • “Try it free” button was changed to “Sign Up Free.”
  • Second screen for the onboarding process was eliminated.
  • “Move Date” selection was changed to “Move Now.”
  • Selections on the left sidebar of the dashboard page were reorganized.
  • Confirmation message was added after the completion of uploading a task.

Branding

The prime mission of this cloud storage platform is to create a space where people can stay connected, help one another, and thrive together. For example, users can connect to family and friends by sharing pictures. Users can also connect with their co-workers by collaborating on projects. I aimed for a simple brand name, so I looked for a self-explanatory title. Once I established the title of the cloud platform, I started to design a logo. It was important to create a simple brand that reflected the idea of connection via sharing files, pictures, and videos. I started with a few logo sketches.


As time went on, I slowly refined the brand into its final composition.

Moodboard

After finalizing the logo, I began to work on the moodboard, which helped me to establish the color palette for this platform. The original moodboard consisted of three primary colors: red, yellow, and blue. However, after constructing the landing page with the primary colors, it became apparent that the colors were not simple to work with afterall. Those colors were very bold and may have caused heightened senses for some users, such as danger due to the red color.

Style Guide

The Connect logo is available in two configurations: black and white. On a light background, the black version must be used and vice versa. The whitespace around the Connect logo should always be equal to the circles within the mark. The font Questrial resembles the quality of simplicity because there is no ear, tail, and link noted, which makes the brand easy to read. In contrast, Oleo Script is a bit more complex and quirky. This idea that opposites attract and that no two individuals are the same make this combination beautiful.

Usability Testing II

After finalizing the clickable prototype, I conducted another usability test with three participants. The same tasks used in the first usability test were performed by three different users. Although I anticipated all three tasks to be completed without any trouble, the task of moving a file into a folder was not completed successfully on the first attempt. Two out of the three users tried to drag “File 1” into “Folder 1.” Nevertheless, all users selected the “Move Now” button once they realized the dragging method had failed.

Lesson Learned

The dragging feature is a method that many users like to use because it’s quick and easy. Also many are now accustomed to this option. When designing the process of completing certain tasks I must do my best to incorporate the dragging feature or the right click method whenever possible. Although I may not be able to test these methods via Figma, I will inform the Web Developer team of these important designs.

Conclusion

Though it would be nice to design a platform that surpasses the current competitors. The most important objective is to create something useful and meaningful. I hope to leave a mark and make a difference in the lives of others.

There’s nothing more rewarding than knowing that via a platform such as The Loft, people are able to reconnect with family and friends during a pandemic where social distancing is crucial.
