The Loft

These are extraordinary times, and countries from all around the world are struggling to keep their economy from crashing while maintaining the health and safety of their fellow citizens. A new video conferencing platform called The Loft has been created to bridge the gap caused by the pandemic.

View Prototype

Objective

Creating a desktop site for a video conferencing platform where meetings and virtual functions can be organized and managed with well-established features.

My Role

UX Researcher, UX Designer

Project Duration

4 weeks

Tools

Figma, Google Forms, Google Sheets

Problems

  • The number of coronavirus cases and deaths are on the rise.
  • The economy is suffering as a result of businesses being forced to shut down.
  • There has been an increase in crime rates and mental health illnesses.
  • Businesses have asked their employees to work remotely from home.
  • Many people are canceling group gatherings to comply with the CDCâ€™s recommendation of social distancing.

Solution

Design a video conferencing platform that is user-friendly and full of interesting features that benefits both working professionals and the general population.

Process

I really enjoyed working on this project because it involved designing a platform that could potentially help mend broken bridges caused by the coronavirus pandemic. I wanted to make sure the site was feasible and versatile for both professional and personal use. Prior to sketching ideas, I devoted a good chunk of my time to researching current competitors and users. Once I learned about them, I began to flesh out the different wireframes and mockups for The Loft. Two rounds of usability testings were performed in this project, allowing potential users to interact with the design. The results gathered were used to modify the design and ultimately improve the overall function of The Loft. Now let me walk you through the different stages.

User Surveys

Before I could start the design process, I needed to collect some valuable data via user surveys. Twenty-one individuals participated in this exercise. The data collected allowed me to evaluate whether or not there is a true need for a video conferencing platform, and what ways people care to use it. I anticipated the top two leading video conferencing platforms would have been Zoom and FaceTime. However, the top two frequently used video conferencing platforms among the 21 participants were Zoom (100%) and Skype (76.2%). I predicted Zoom would be the main video conferencing platform used for personal use and Cisco WebEx would be the main platform used for work. However, Zoom turned out to be the leading video conferencing platform for both personal use and work. There has been an increase in the use of video conferencing platforms since the rise of COVID-19, which supports the growing need for a video conferencing platform such as The Loft.

Results

  • Before the start of COVID-19, roughly 24% of participants used video conferencing platforms for more than an hour.
  • After the rise of COVID-19, roughly 67% of participants use video conferencing platforms for more than an hour.
  • The top 3 features: sharing screens (76.2%), mute feature (52.4%), turn off camera (52.4%).
  • The top 3 video conferencing platforms are Zoom, Skype, and Google Hangouts.
  • 100% of my participants do not pay a monthly fee to use their video conferencing platform.
  • 71.4% of participants use Zoom and 14.3% of participants use Skype for personal use.
  • 52.4% of participants use Zoom and 19% of participants use Cisco WebEx/Appear.in at work.
  • 95.2% of participants use a laptop to access a video conferencing platform.

Take Away Message

Users value free storage space on the cloud platform. They mostly use the platform for personal use and/or work. Many users share pictures and documents with other people through cloud platforms.

Usability Testing I

Once I finished the digital wireframes in Figma, I made a clickable prototype that would later be used to perform usability testing on three potential users. Each participant was asked to complete the 5 tasks listed below. The tasks chosen were based upon the survey participants' response towards the top features they used in video conferencing platforms.

  1. Sign up for an account using an email address and a password.
  2. Log out from the homepage and then log in using email address and password.
  3. Schedule a meeting using the participantâ€™s email address.
  4. Mute yourself (name: Derrick Brown) during a conference call.
  5. Share screen 2 with colleagues.

In the beginning, I predicted all three participants would have difficulties with scheduling a meeting simply because there are several buttons on the dashboard. However, that was not the case. In fact, all three participants successfully completed task 1, 2, 3, & 5 on their first try. For task 4 (mute yourself), one participant clicked the microphone, which is found in the right sidebar beside the name Derrick Brown, instead of selecting the microphone button located at the bottom. This was no mistake of the participant, but rather of the designer. I had forgotten to include this route when producing the clickable prototype.

Lessons Learned from Usability Testing I

  • Potential users can help a designer pick-up on a mistake and/or uncover new ways for completing a task
  • Users can navigate quickly on sites that seem familiar to them.
  • Apart from colors, images also play a role in creating a particular vibe/mood on a site.

Updates Made to Wireframe

Clickable prototype updated to allow a user to mute himself/herself by clicking on the microphone found in the right sidebar while on a video call.

Usability Testing II

Another group of individuals participated in the second round of usability testing. All three potential users completed the five tasks successfully. However, two out of the three participants failed to correctly answer what the site was about within the first 30 seconds of being on the landing page. The participants focused first on pictures before looking at the words on a page, which explains why many designers place words on top of an image. This method is very effective because it forces the users to direct their attention to the text on a page.

Lesson Learned

  • Users look at images before texts.
  • Users donâ€™t care to read much on a landing page; they mostly scan through it all.

Updates Made to High-Fidelity Mockup

  • On the landing page, words with high importance were bolded.
  • On the landing page, text describing The Loft was moved to the top of an image.
